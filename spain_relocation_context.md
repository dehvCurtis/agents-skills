## Beckham Law — Tax Overview

### What It Is
Spain's Special Tax Regime for Impatriate Workers (Article 93 LIRPF), commonly called the Beckham Law, allows qualifying expats to pay a flat 24% tax rate on Spanish-sourced income up to €600,000 for up to 6 years, instead of Spain's standard progressive rates of 19–47%.

### Key 2026 Facts
- Flat rate: **24%** on income up to €600,000
- Duration: **6 years**
- DNV holders explicitly confirmed eligible under Law 28/2022 (Startups Act)
- No changes to Beckham Law announced for 2026
- 2027 tweaks under consultation (EU Minimum Wage Directive) — monitor

### Eligibility Requirements
- Must not have been a Spanish tax resident in the **previous 5 tax years**
- Must relocate to Spain for professional/work reasons
- Must perform at least **85% of work within Spain**
- Must apply within **6 months of registering with Spanish Social Security**
- DNV holders are an explicitly listed eligible category

### 2025 Ruling (Carrying Into 2026)
A July 2025 TEAC ruling confirmed Beckham Law beneficiaries must pay imputed rental income tax on their Spanish primary residence (2% of cadastral value). Since Dehvon will be **renting**, not owning, in Alicante — this is a non-issue.
